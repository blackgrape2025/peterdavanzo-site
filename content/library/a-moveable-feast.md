# 



*A Moveable Feast* is Hemingway's memoir of Paris. It was a city of warm cafes, ambitious young writers, cold winters, and long conversations.

I love its clarity. Hemingway strips away explanation and leaves just the concrete details: the street below his window, a meal, a room, a feeling. 

Hemingway called this the "Iceberg Theory". The most important thing is often left unsaid. The surface is simple, but the emotional weight lies underneath.

I am drawn to his approach in 2026. We live in an age where everything is explained. Hemingway reminds me that meaning often emerges sideways.
